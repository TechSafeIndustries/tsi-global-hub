The Aviation Reality

Common failure modes in aviation today

  • Incidents and hazard reports captured but not consistently closed across airside, maintenance, and ground ops
  • SMS (Safety Management System) workflows fragmented across CASA and ICAO requirements
  • Aircraft maintenance corrective actions lost across shifts and contractors
  • Ground handling and airside safety assurance conducted retrospectively
  • Executives lack defensible visibility across multi-site aviation operations

This describes risk exposure, not technology failure.

Example Governed Aviation Workflows

SMS: Incident & Hazard Reporting

  • Incident or hazard logged from airside, maintenance, or ground ops (mobile/web)
  • Severity and consequence assessed per SMS framework
  • Investigation conducted with evidence (photos, statements, telemetry)
  • Actions assigned, approved, and closed with SMS traceability
  • Full audit trail for CASA scrutiny

Aircraft Maintenance: CAMO/CAME Workflows

  • Defect logged with aircraft registration and MEL/CDL assessment
  • Corrective action assigned with CASA Part 145 compliance
  • Work executed with evidence (certifications, photos, sign-offs)
  • Aircraft return-to-service with approval and evidence trail

Ground Handling & Airside Safety

  • Airside Permit-to-Work issued with isolation and safety controls
  • FOD (Foreign Object Debris) reporting with escalation rules
  • Ground support equipment (GSE) defects logged and governed
  • Evidence trails retained for Part 139 compliance

Contractor Oversight

  • Contractor incidents and actions governed under airside authority
  • Role-based access enforced (maintenance, ground ops, security)
  • No uncontrolled closure or escalation
